Chakolya

Chakolya is a Maharashtrian recipe, also known as "Dal Dhokli". The concept is to make spicy dal (Amti) and cook the small squares of uncooked wheat chapati in the same.

Ingredients

  1. Toor dal: 1/2 up
  2. Wheat flour: 1.5 - 2 cups
  3. Dry coconut - grated: 1/4 cup
  4. Garlic: 3-4 buds
  5. Cumin seeds: 1/2 tablespoon
  6. Mustard seeds: 1/2 tablespoon
  7. Maharashtrian Goda Masala: 1.5 Tablespoons
  8. Corriander leaves (Chopped): 1/4 bunch
  9. Red chili powder: to taste
  10. Turmeric
  11. Salt: to taste
  12. Water: as needed
  13. Oil: 2 Tablespoons

Procedure

  1. Cook toor dal with a pinch of turmeric. If using an instant pot, 10 minutes of high pressure cooking is required.
  2. Finely grind garlic and cumin seeds.
  3. In a Kadhai or pan, heat oil, add Mustard seeds for tempering.
  4. Once mustard seeds start spluttering, add the coconut paste. Roast until it turns golden brownish.
  5. Add salt, chili powder, Goda Masala and stir fry the mixture.
  6. Add cooked dal and Corriander leaves. Mix the Ingredients and add 2-3 cups of water so that the dal becomes thinner
  7. Put a lid and let it boil.
  8. In the meanwhite, add little water to Wheat flour and mix it together to make a soft dough, like pizza dough. Apply little oil and keep it aside in a box.
  9. When the dal comes to boil, roll small balls of wheat dough into a chapati. Make small squares/ diamond like shapes using pizza cutter.
  10. Keeping dal on lower flame.Gently float the chapati squares into the dal.
  11. Add all the chapati squares in a similar way.
  12. Boil the chakolya on a low flame for 10-15 minutes.
  13. Chakolya are ready to be served with ghee!
